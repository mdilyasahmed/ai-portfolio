Professional Profile

With a strong commitment to advancing patient safety, I am a Pharmacovigilance professional with progressive experience supporting global drug safety across investigational and marketed products.

Expertise & Experience

My work spans the drug development lifecycle, where I contribute to safety science and regulatory deliverables that uphold high standards of patient safety, scientific rigor, and compliance. Drawing on hands-on experience in clinical safety and pharmacovigilance, I support and lead safety activities including safety surveillance, signal detection, aggregate reporting, risk management planning, and health authority interactions. I bring deep working knowledge of global pharmacovigilance frameworks, including ICH guidelines, FDA and EMA regulatory requirements, with hands-on experience contributing to targeted health authority responses across multiple programs.

Professional Strengths

Throughout my roles, I am recognized as a self-starter with the ability to pivot in fast-paced environments, proactively identifying gaps, driving complex safety workstreams forward, and delivering high-quality outputs with minimal direction. I have experience synthesizing clinical trial, post-marketing, and real-world safety data into clear, actionable insights, and I am known for showing initiative, influencing cross-functional stakeholders, and communicating effectively to support data-driven decision-making.

Collaborative Approach

I value collaboration and work closely with clinical, regulatory, epidemiology, biostatistics, data management, and operational partners to ensure alignment and scientific integrity across safety deliverables. Highly organized and detail-oriented, I bring a practical, solutions-focused mindset to complex safety issues while maintaining a strong focus on patient impact and regulatory excellence.

Vision & Purpose

I am motivated by opportunities to continue growing as a safety scientist and leader, contributing meaningfully to programs that improve patient outcomes and advance the development of innovative therapies.
